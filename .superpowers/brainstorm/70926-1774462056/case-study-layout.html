Dan Koe Case Study — Page Layout

How should the five acts flow? The blueprint prescribes content — this is about pacing and visual weight.

A

Cinematic Scroll — Full Bleed Acts

Each act is a full-screen section (100vh). Act 1 hero fills the viewport — just the name, hook, and void. The visitor arrives in the case study, then scrolls through five distinct rooms. Maximum atmosphere, maximum drama. Matches the homepage feel exactly.

Tradeoff: Long scroll. Acts 3 (architecture phases) and 4 (delta bridge rows) can feel buried deep in the page.
B

Editorial Scroll — Variable Height Acts

Act 1 is tall and cinematic (80-100vh). Acts 2–5 are content-height — they expand to fit what's in them, not a fixed viewport. Feels like reading a long-form essay that starts with presence and then flows into depth. Most natural reading rhythm.

Tradeoff: Less cinematic after Act 1. The dramatic pacing relaxes once the content starts.
C

Act Navigation — Sticky Progress

A fixed left-rail (or top indicator) shows the five acts as you scroll: ① ② ③ ④ ⑤. Each act is full-height. The visitor always knows where they are in the loop. Adds a layer of wayfinding — the journey IS the product.

Tradeoff: More complex to build. The left rail competes with the content for attention. Risk of feeling like a timeline rather than a narrative.
